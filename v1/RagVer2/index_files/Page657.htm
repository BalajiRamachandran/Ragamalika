Mrs Maragatham Ramaswamy

Ragamalika is proud to present Mrs Maragatham Ramaswamy, the founder and guiding spirit of Ragamalika. Her dedication, devotion and skill and knowledge as well as her loving teaching style has endeared her to one and all.

Maragatham Ramaswamy was formally educated and trained in Carnatic music as a vocalist and a violinist. Earning the title of Sangeetha Vidhwan at an early age in these two pursuits, she has performed at prestigious sabhas in Madras and at All India Radio, Madras before she moved to the United States in the 1980s with her family. 

 

Education  and Training

Maragatham started training as a violinist at a very young age and then later added vocal music to her repertoire.

· She graduated from the prestigious Music College of Madras with a Gold Medal for outstanding achievement.

· The title Sangeeta Vidhwan was conferred on Maragatham for violin and vocal music in April 1963 by the Government of Madras (Tamil Nadu).

· She was honored by "Tamil Thenral Radio" for her outstanding contribution and achievement to Carnatic Music in the Washington D.C. metropolitan area.

· She auditioned and was accepted as an All India Radio artiste for violin and vocal performances, at AIR, Madras.

· Maragatham Ramaswamy has received many awards from prestigious organizations such as the Music Academy and the Krishna Gana Sabha for her vocal and violin performances.

· Prominent music critics in major Indian newspapers (Hindu, Indian Express, Ananda Vikatan) have recognized Maragatham for her musicianship.

· In 1994, Maragatham was the music consultant for Terrence McNally's "A  Perfect Ganesh" performed at the Arena Stage in Washington D.C.

· Maragatham and Mrs. Usha Char have released a double cassette album containing rare compositions on  the Hindu deities 'Vinayaka' and 'Devi'.

Violin

· Maragatham comes from a musical family. She grew up listening to her sister Kalaimamani Radha Narayanan playing the violin.

· She received her initial training under Trivandrum V. Harihara Iyer

· Later she honed her skills under the guidance of famous musicians like T.N.Krishnan, M.S. Anantharaman and Lalgudi Jayaraman.

Vocal

· Maragatham started her vocal training under a renowned vocalist, the late S. Kalayanaraman, a disciple of the late maestro G.N. Balasubramaniam.

· She has also received vocal training from prominent musicians such as the late Palghat K.V.Narayanaswamy, T.M.Thiagarajan, and Ramnad Krishnan.
